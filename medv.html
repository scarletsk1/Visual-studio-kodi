The School of Athens

The School of Athens is a masterpiece of Renaissance art painted by Raphael between 1509 and 1511. This fresco symbolizes philosophy and celebrates the intellectual achievements of classical antiquity, reflecting the Renaissance ideals of humanism and knowledge.

The painting features a grand architectural setting inspired by ancient Roman and Greek designs. At the center stand Plato and Aristotle, symbolizing different schools of thought: Plato points upward to the realm of ideal forms, while Aristotle gestures horizontally to emphasize empirical knowledge. Surrounding them are other great thinkers, including Socrates, Pythagoras, and Euclid, depicted in dynamic poses that reflect their intellectual pursuits.

Mona Lisa

The Mona Lisa, painted by Leonardo da Vinci between 1503 and 1506, is one of the most famous and iconic works of art in history. It is celebrated for its revolutionary artistic techniques and the mystery surrounding its subject's enigmatic smile. The painting depicts Lisa Gherardini, the wife of a Florentine merchant, although her exact identity is still debated.

The Mona Lisa embodies Renaissance ideals of humanism and naturalism, focusing on individual identity and the subtle interplay of emotion and expression. It has captivated audiences for centuries and remains a symbol of artistic genius and cultural significance.

The Last Supper

The Last Supper, painted by Leonardo da Vinci between 1495 and 1498, is one of the most renowned works of the Renaissance. It depicts the dramatic moment when Jesus announces to his disciples that one of them will betray him.The emotional expressions and gestures of the disciples convey shock, disbelief, and confusion, making the scene dynamic and full of life. Leonardo's use of light and shadow enhances the realism and drama of the work.

Though originally painted using an experimental technique combining tempera and oil on dry plaster, the fresco has faced significant deterioration over the centuries. Despite this, The Last Supper remains a testament to Leonardo's genius and his ability to blend art, emotion, and storytelling in a single masterpiece.
